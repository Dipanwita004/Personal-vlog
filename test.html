Trip to the Mountains

Date: August 5, 2024 | Author: Dipanwita Acharya

The quaint village of Mawlynnong, located approximately 90 km away from the capital city of Shillong, Meghalaya. The village may be small in size, and has a total population of less than 1000 individuals, but it is one of the most memorable places you will ever visit in India. This charming village offers visitors a pristine environment, which is already very difficult to come by these days. Like most places in Meghalaya, Mawlynnong also enjoys pleasant temperatures throughout the year. Add to that the quiet, almost idyllic surrounding, and you have one of the most beautiful holidays. Almost poetic in fact.

Jungle Saffari

Date: July 20, 2024 | Author: Dipanwita Acharya

Jim Corbett National Park is a renowned wildlife sanctuary in India, offering visitors a unique opportunity to experience the beauty and diversity of nature. With its lush forests, diverse flora and fauna, and the thrill of spotting majestic tigers, it's a destination that appeals to nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. Here's a summary of common reviews and experiences: Pros: * Wildlife Encounters: The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including tigers, elephants, leopards, and various bird species. Many visitors report thrilling sightings and unforgettable experiences. * Scenic Beauty: The park's natural beauty is breathtaking, with lush forests, rivers, and picturesque landscapes.

Tropical Paradise

Date: June 15, 2024 | Author: Dipanwita Acharya

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands offer a stunning blend of pristine beaches, lush forests, and vibrant marine life. These remote islands, located in the Bay of Bengal, are a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
Key Attractions and Activities:
* Pristine Beaches: Enjoy the soft sands and crystal-clear waters of Radhanagar Beach on Havelock Island, often ranked among the best beaches in Asia.
* Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: Explore vibrant coral reefs and encounter diverse marine life, including colorful fish, turtles, and even sharks.
* Trekking: Embark on nature trails through the dense tropical forests and discover hidden waterfalls and wildlife.
* Water Sports: Enjoy activities like kayaking, jet skiing, and parasailing on the calm waters.
* Wildlife: Spot various bird species, including the endemic Andaman Woodpecker, and observe the elusive Andaman Pig.
Best Time to Visit:
* November to April: This is the peak tourist season, offering pleasant weather and ideal conditions for water activities.
* May to October: While the monsoon season brings heavy rainfall, it also offers lower prices and fewer crowds.
